On Licenses

ZT1 Mod Install Guide ZT2 Mod Install Guide

Every file on ZooBerry contains a license field. If no license is disclosed, you can assume the file is a closed work and not available for derivation or redistribution. Many authors do choose to release their under an open source license, such as public domain. If you’re an author and wish to find out more about what license you might pick so submit to ZooBerry, please read ahead.

Zoo Tycoon Public Domain

The Zoo Tycoon community has for many years regulated a self-imposed licensing mechanism that allows creators to establish whether their mod is to be released under public domain or not. To be public domain within the context of the Zoo Tycoon modding scene is to say that an author’s “content, meaning coding, model or mesh, skin and/or animations may be used to create new content and release it, as long as credit is given to the original creator.”

The license definition page continues with a ‘subflavor’ of the main license: “A download can also be partly Public Domain when only some content, for example only a skin or only a model, can be used in this fashion.”

Source: https://zt2downloadlibrary.fandom.com/wiki/Public_Domain

When submitting files to ZooBerry, I do not want to discourage anyone from picking the license of their choice. However, I do understand that there have been disagreements in the past when a file released under public domain by its author would contest derivative work under certain circumstances. Though it is not my place to enforce their terms, I would encourage these authors to take a look at other open source licenses available that might prove a bit more flexible for their needs–they might provide a bit more clarity to them, and to their users. Below are resources they can use to help them pick such a license.

Please take the time to visit https://choosealicense.com/. It is designed specifically for someone who wishes to publish their work with a certain framework in mind, and can help you get there quickly.

Q&A

Why use an open source license?

There are many licenses out there and long-since established. If you’re excited to share your work for free and in open spirit to allow for derivative work, it might be worth looking into protecting your hard work within the boundaries you mean to establish even inside of an open source space.

What if you don’t pick a license?

That’s okay! If you don’t pick a license, your work is automatically protected by copyright law and is yours. However, by not declaring a license you are implicitly declaring that your work is not open source and not available for derivative work. Unfortunately, since so many of the legacy mods here on ZooBerry were released before modders started giving their works a license, they remain closed works.

In Summary

What is most important to remember about picking an open source license is that you are committing your work for the public to use in some way, shape, or form. Please make sure to understand your own terms to avoid any possible miscommunication with your users.
